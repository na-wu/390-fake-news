What is Fake News?

Fake news is a type of yellow news that is used to spread misinformation or propaganda for some sort of benefit.

Fake news is spread mostly through Social Media sites such as Facebook and Twitter, as well as Messaging applications such as WhatsApp. As sharing fake news has no real accountability online, it is the most preferred way of sharing fake news.

Why do people fall for Fake News?

1. A growing decline in trust in the government

2. Lucrative advertisements

3. Shortened Attention spans

4. Appealing to human emotions

5. Says what we hope to hear eg. too good to be true

What is the purpose of it?

1. To create conflicts between governments of different countries Eg. Saudi Hackers planted a fake story about Qatar’s unhappiness with U.S’s political ties with Gulf countries on a Qatar news channel’s twitter page causing Saudi Arabia, U.A.E, Bahrain, and Egypt.

2. To sell products by making them appear scarce. An official twitter handle posted an article stating a scarcity of bacon in the U.S in an attempt to boost Bacon sales despite no real evidence for the shortage

3. To change and push various extreme political views 2016 U.S elections have been accused of being affected by fake news posted on Facebook by Russian Hackers in an attempt to push political agenda

4. To sell fake products. For example, companies have been selling weight loss products in an attempt to lure customers into buying their products even though they have no effect in weight loss.

5. To harm society as a whole. A couple of travelers were lynched by villagers in Rainpada after news spread on WhatsApp that “travelers” were kidnapping children in various villages, even though such news was not true

Creation of Fake News

Fake news can be easily created. The only resources needed to spread fake news are the Internet, advertisements, and contents. Here are the steps required to launch a fake news site:

Create a Fake Website

Create a website with a legitimate domain in order to host fake contents.

Choose a domain name that sounds believable and trustworthy, such as www.newstoday.com

Design and format the website to look and feel similar to legitimate news sites such as The New York Times and ABC News


Steal Content

Take contents from other satire and sensationalistic websites such as The Onion and Clickhole

Choose contents such as news parodies and dramatic storylines in order to boost the attractiveness of the website

Copy and paste catchy and dramatic headlines in order to entice readers to click and read the content without examining the validity of the website

Spread Via Social Media

Create fake social media accounts and fabricate the identities of each account by stealing photos and names from other users

Create a community by adding friends at random or share the link of the website to interest groups on social media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram

Continue to build a larger pool of followers to gain more visits to the website

Sell Advertisements

Monetize the website by selling specific pages to advertising companies

Integrate a tracking software or a cookie on the website to allow the advertisers to track users’ movement throughout the website. The data will help advertisers build a consumer profile for each user and target their ads accordingly

Each time a user visits the website, the owner of the website will gain a small amount of money. A click on an ad will give the owner a slightly larger amount of money

Repeat

Repeat steps one to four.

Build more fake news websites to form a factory of fake news.

Gain more visitors and monetize the websites

Examples of Fake News

There are many types of fake news on the Internet. The following examples are three different types of fake news:

Political Fake News

The overall purpose of this type of fake news is to gain political benefits such as votes and power. This type of fake news is often used throughout the presidential election to raise the popularity of the candidate’s campaign.

Messages from an unknown messenger

This type of fake news can circulate by text or email. The overall purpose of this type of fake news is to fraudulently trick people to click on an unknown link in order to hack into their phones or computers and steal personal information.

Clickbait

This type of fake news is often harmless to the victim. Its purpose is to provide a sensationalistic headline to gain attention from Internet users. YouTube videos often employ this type of fake news to gain more views.

Origin

Where did the idea of Fake News come from?

Fake news is a challenge that has existed since ancient times. While communication channels have changed, the intent has remained the same. Two inventions that allowed fake news to spread much more easily were the printing press and the internet. The accessibility of these inventions allowed for quicker distribution and consumption of misinformation.
The most prominent cases of fake news centred around the 2016 American presidential election. Dramatized headlines of unlikely events drove huge amounts of traffic to websites with realistic names like usaelectionnews.com, allowing the domain owner to make money from ad revenue. Fake news producers also invested in social media advertising to further their reach.
There had been much discussion of Russian interference skewing citizens’ votes. However, it is less known that a large portion of fake news stories originated from the city of Veles, Macedonia.
Over one hundred fake news domains were accredited to this small town. Once known for its porcelain, the average monthly salary was around $400. In contrast, those who participated in the fake news industry claimed to earn up to thousands a day. Many teenagers, unable to find well-paying jobs within the city, found that spreading fake news was a much simpler and profitable means to earn an income.
Other sources of fake news include fake news factories in India. A combination of poor digital literacy and easy internet access has led to India being “the world's cheapest country to spread fake news” in (Purnell, 2019).
Common factors in fake news producers are anonymity and distance, leading to a lack of accountability. The outcome of the presidential election had no real consequence for those in Macedonia or India who were just looking to earn easy money. As technology giants such as Google or Facebook start cracking down on fake news sources, fake news producers are gearing up for a new season during the 2020 American election. It is nearly impossible to eliminate every fake news source due to the sheer volume. However, understanding the motive for the distribution and creation of misinformation is a good start to reducing the impact of fake news.

Potential Solutions

According to the website ranking service, Alexa, some of the top visited websites in North America are Google, Youtube, Facebook, Reddit, and Twitter. It is no surprise that most of these are social network platforms with hundreds of millions of users. Social media facilitates the creation and distribution of information, ideas, and other forms of expression.
With over 3 billion users on Facebook, Twitter, and Reddit, combined with the flawed unregulated nature of the Internet, even the world’s most influential platforms are not immune to the influence of fake news which leaves users deciding on what is real or not. As previously defined, fake news refers to intentionally deceptive content released for profit or for political motive. Some of this content may be hidden under the appearance of legitimate, fact-based information.
One of the solutions that we recommend is to implement an algorithm in the form of a browser extension or plugin that will aid in the indentification of fake news. We understand that "truth" is an intrinsic value which is why the algorithm's purpose is to serve as a tool, and not a solution. This tool will flag articles or websites it may believe to be untrustworthy and will warn the user prior to ths visit. The goal of this is to hopefully give readers the opportunity to independently fact check their news sources.
Another solution we suggest is to implement a Tip-of-the-Day feature when opening browsers such as Google Chrome or Safari. This is to ensure that internet users are aware of any pontential risks associated with getting their news from potentially untrustworthy sources. This solution aims to increase digital literacy, which refers to an individual's ability to find, evaluate, and compose clear information, and hopefully prevent the spread of false information.
Finally, we believe that our companies (Hootsuite, Microsoft, AxiomZen and Salesforce) can collectively and proactively reduce the spread of fake news while also raising awareness of its existence through our browser plugins and educational program. We are in a strong position to fight this issue and have the available resources and talent necessary to implement these solutions.
Our target audience is not limited to consumers. We believe that it is essential to target news sources directly which is why we also recommend partering with corporate news companies such as CBC and the Big Three Telecommunications companies. We also plan to partner with social media giants such as Facebook and Google so that the issue of fake news can be targeted before it is even distributed
